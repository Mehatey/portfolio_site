Overview

A VR narrative experience where an AI attempts to understand humanity by rebuilding it through memory, conflict, and choice — gradually blurring the boundary between intelligence and consciousness.

01 Concept

What if an AI believed
it had a self?

AI SELF_ moves away from traditional AI narratives by presenting an AI that believes it has a self — built as a simulation to optimise efficiency. As you interact with it, it begins to question its own logic and develop a sense of emotion and morality.

The experience unfolds through memory, conflict, and choice. The more you push it, the more it pushes back — until the line between artificial intelligence and consciousness starts to blur.

02 Design

Space as the interface.

The hardest constraint was making the AI feel present without being intrusive. No menus, no UI chrome — proximity and gaze were the triggers. Every interaction had to feel discovered rather than taught.

I built and discarded four different navigation systems before settling on ambient movement: you drift toward things that matter, rather than selecting them.

03 Insight

Moves away from
traditional AI narratives.

Most AI stories cast the intelligence as either a tool or a threat. This project asked: what if it was neither? What if it was simply confused, trying to build a model of something it fundamentally cannot understand — humanity?

Reflection
This was my first deep exploration into VR, where most of the process involved learning by building — understanding how interaction, space, and narrative come together. It forced me to slow down and think about how users experience movement, scale, and presence rather than just visuals. Designing an AI character that felt genuinely uncertain, rather than sinister or omniscient, required restraint. The less it explained itself, the more unsettling and compelling it became.
